Planetary Habitability, Global Convergence and Structural Transformation, 2026–2100

Lucas Chancel, Cornelia Mohren, Moritz Odersky, Thomas Piketty, Anmol Somanchi — 2026

We develop a welfare-theoretic framework to evaluate two long-run global development pathways over 2025–2100. The Sustainable Convergence scenario achieves broad-based convergence to high living standards through structural transformation, shorter working hours, and rapid decarbonisation, reaching approximately 0.4°C of cumulative warming by 2100. The Productivist Convergence scenario achieves higher raw output growth but with longer working hours and 2.8°C of cumulative warming.

Going beyond GDP, we value both consumption and leisure, and apply an exponential climate damage wedge that reduces effective consumption as temperatures rise. Welfare is compared using a consumption equivalent metric across eight world regions. Sustainable Convergence is welfare-superior in every region. The global welfare advantage is equivalent to an 8.4% permanent increase in consumption under baseline parameters, and is robust across utility specifications, discount rates, and damage parameterisations.

Framework

Scenarios and Data

Each scenario provides annual region-level projections (2025–2100) of per-capita consumption, per-capita labour hours, and temperature increments for eight world regions: Europe, North America & Oceania, Latin America, MENA, Sub-Saharan Africa, Russia & Central Asia, East Asia, and South & South-East Asia.

Climate Damage Wedge

Effective consumption is defined as raw consumption multiplied by an exponential damage factor exp[−(φ + γ)ΔT], where φ = 12% captures output damages and γ = 13.33% captures non-market amenity losses (health, ecosystems, infrastructure) per degree of warming. At 2.8°C, effective consumption is roughly halved relative to raw output.

Welfare Metric

Period utility values both damage-adjusted consumption and leisure (time endowment minus working hours). Lifetime welfare is the discounted sum of period utilities. The consumption equivalent λ is the permanent percentage change in consumption that would make an individual under Productivist Convergence indifferent to Sustainable Convergence.
